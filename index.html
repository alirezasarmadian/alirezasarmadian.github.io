OBJECTIVE

I am a a Research Fellow (on a Global Talent Visa) and Chancellor's Award-winning PhD student at the University of Sussex. I came to Sussex in the Autumn of 2018. On arrival, I joined a team of 6 people at Sussex completing an EPSRC-funded project in collaboration with Ford, Ricardo, and Denso. My research has been on the use of an evaporative spray cooling system for cooling automotive electrical and electronic powertrain components.
The particular areas where I have made a contribution to the research are on experimental data collection, construction of very novel dynamic correlation models, development by simulation of a robust spray evaporative cooling control system, and implementation and testing in hardware of a robust spray evaporative cooling control system. This work contributed to a 9-month extension of the project from May 2019 to January 2020, funded by Ricardo plc, and has included me communicating my research, and playing a leading part in producing journal papers. I have submitted my thesis on 23rd April 2021.
I have also passed the Associate Fellow of the Higher Education Academy (AFHEA) assessment (fellowship reference number: PR190187). Since February 2019, I have been working as a Doctoral Tutor at the University of Sussex involved in teaching activities.
I plan to develop my career, preferably in academia such as postdoctoral research, research fellowship and lecturership. I intend to continue my research in thermal engineering, in particular, the implementation of advanced thermal management systems for automotive and aerospace applications.
As a thermal engineer expert in theoretical and experimental heat transfer, research and development work on zero-emission vehicles is indeed an area of interest to me. During my academic roles, I want to write proposals on the thermal management system design of evaporative cooling methods for applications in motor and aerospace vehicles. Applications including but not limited to power electronic components (e.g. cooling high-power LEDs, IGBTs, high-heat flux heat sinks, and micro-electro-mechanical systems in aerospace), Lithium-ion batteries (for the specific purpose of improving thermal performance or to achieve optimised lifespan), and hydrogen technologies (cooling storage tanks, catalyst surface temperature control for hydrogen fuel cells). My interest in using environmentally-friendly coolants (with low to zero global warming potential) in my cooling system designs will be helpful in protecting our natural environment.
Recently, I've started a 3-month post of Research Fellow (part-time) in the Department of Engineering and Design to work on a novel resonating free-piston generator concept. The post focuses on control engineering development to manage key mechanical and electrical machine functions of the generator. This involves control system design, simulation, and rapid prototyping; build, test, and hardware demonstration.
